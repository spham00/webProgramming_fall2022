Greetings

There are many ways to greet someone in Korean. The following ways are organized in the below table! Note that all phrases use the -yo (-요), which is standard formality.

English Hangul Romanization
Hello 안녕하세요 an-nyeong-ha-se-yo
Did you sleep well? 잘 잤어요? jal ja-sseo-yo?
Long time no see 오랜만이에요 o-raen-man-i-e-yo
How are you? 잘 지냈어요? jal ji-nae-sseo-yo?
Have you eaten yet? 밥 먹었어요? bap meo-geo-sseo-yo?

Some Basics

The table below demonstrates basic phrases that you need to know for daily life. These are uncategorized and the formality differs from casual to standard to formal.

English Hangul Romanization
Yes (agree) 네 ne
No (disagree) 아니요 a-ni-yo
Thank you 고맙습니다 go-map-seum-ni-da
I don't know 몰라요 mo-lla-yo
Sorry 미안합니다 mi-an-ham-ni-da
Goodbye 안녕히 가세요 an-nyeong-hi ga-se-yo

Learn More

Here are some tips I've collected on how to study Korean!

  • Listen to K-pop or other Korean music.
  • Watch Korean media like K-dramas.
  • Practice with someone who is fluent.
  • Learn the alphabet when you can (only takes 30 min).
  • Check out free resources and sites.
  • There also may be videos/courses on YouTube.
  • If you can, live in a Korean-speaking environment.
