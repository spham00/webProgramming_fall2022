Jupiter

Below are some fun facts I've gathered about Jupiter from other sites. These sources include Universe Today and Nine Planets.

Jupiter is the fastest spinning planet in the solar system, spinning at 45,300km/h, or 28,148 mph.
Jupiter has 79 moons. Most of them are not very big (10km), but still, there are a lot. The most prominent ones are known as the Galilean Moons.
Jupiter is the biggest planet in the solar system and is bigger than all the other planets combined (almost by 2x).
Jupiter is like a magnet for comets and asteroids, attracting them with its gravity, which in a way protects other planets in the solar system from being hit by them.
Jupiter is a gas giant, being made up of hydrogen and helium for the most part.
One year on Jupiter is 11.8 Earth years because of how far away it is from the Sun, and the days only last 10 hours.
